# Aqeedah Reference — Manhaj Salaf

Core creed (aqeedah) topics within the Salafi methodology.

> **Important distinction:** The Shafi'i preference in this skill applies to **fiqh (jurisprudence)** only. In **aqeedah (creed)**, the Salafi methodology is followed exclusively — based on Al-Quran, As-Sunnah, and the understanding of the Salafush Shalih. Imam Shafi'i himself (rahimahullah) was firmly upon the aqeedah of Ahlus Sunnah wal Jama'ah.

---

## TAWHID (Monotheism) — The Foundation of Islam

### Three Categories of Tawhid
1. **Tawhid Ar-Rububiyyah** — Oneness of Allah's Lordship: affirming that Allah alone is the Creator, Sustainer, and Controller of all affairs
2. **Tawhid Al-Uluhiyyah** — Oneness of Worship: all acts of worship must be directed to Allah alone
3. **Tawhid Al-Asma' was Sifat** — Oneness of Names and Attributes: affirming Allah's names and attributes as they appear in the texts, without distortion (tahrif), negation (ta'til), resemblance to creation (tamthil), or asking "how" (takyif)

**Key source:** Kitab At-Tawhid by Shaykh Muhammad bin Abdul Wahhab (rahimahullah)

---

## SHIRK (Associating Partners with Allah)

### Categories
- **Shirk Akbar (Major Shirk)** — Exits the person from Islam. Examples: supplicating to other than Allah, making vows to graves, seeking intercession through the deceased
- **Shirk Asghar (Minor Shirk)** — Does not exit from Islam but carries severe warning. Examples: riya' (showing off in worship), swearing by other than Allah

### Common Modern Forms
- Visiting graves and making du'a to the deceased (tawassul through the dead)
- Wearing amulets (ta'wiz/jimat) with the belief they have inherent power
- Seeking ruqyah from practitioners using non-Quranic or impermissible methods

**Key fatwa:** Lajnah Da'imah (alifta.net); Shaykh Ibn Baz (binbaz.org.sa)

## BID'AH (Religious Innovation)

### Definition
Introducing into the religion anything that Allah and His Messenger did not legislate, whether in belief or practice.

**Daleel:** The Prophet ﷺ said: *"Every innovation is misguidance, and every misguidance is in the Fire."* (HR. An-Nasa'i — graded sahih by Al-Albani)

### Common Bid'ah in Indonesia
- **Tahlilan** — Commemoration gatherings held on the 3rd, 7th, 40th, or 100th day after a death
- **Maulid celebrations** with specific rituals that have no authenticated basis in the Sunnah
- **Qunut Nazilah** recited in all five daily prayers without a specific calamity
- **Congregational dhikr** after obligatory prayers with a designated leader and fixed count

**Reference:** Almanhaj.or.id; Muslim.or.id; Ustaz Yazid bin Abdul Qadir Jawas (rahimahullah)

## AHLUS SUNNAH WAL JAMA'AH — Correct Understanding

The "saved group" (firqah najiyah) refers not to any specific organization, but to those who follow:
1. The Quran
2. The authentic Sunnah
3. Upon the understanding of the Sahabah (Companions of the Prophet ﷺ)

This is a methodology, not a party or movement.

---

## IMAN (Faith) — Its Pillars and Nature

### The Six Pillars of Iman
1. Belief in Allah
2. Belief in His Angels
3. Belief in His Books
4. Belief in His Messengers
5. Belief in the Last Day
6. Belief in Qadar (Divine Decree) — both its good and its evil

### Iman Increases and Decreases
The Salafi position: Iman increases through acts of obedience and decreases through sins. This is the agreed-upon position of the Salaf, in opposition to the Murji'ah who claim iman does not fluctuate.

---

## AL-WALA' WAL BARA' (Loyalty and Disavowal)

- **Al-Wala'** — Love for Allah, His Messenger ﷺ, and the believers; supporting them
- **Al-Bara'** — Disavowal of shirk, kufr, and bid'ah — directed at the falsehood itself, not necessarily at individuals as people

**Important distinction:** Disavowing kufr and innovation does not mean being unjust to non-Muslims or preventing da'wah being given to them. Muslims must still deal with others fairly and with good character.
