# Muamalah Reference — Islamic Transactions (Manhaj Salaf)

Specialist scholar: **Ustaz Erwandi Tarmizi** | Primary reference: PengusahaMuslim.com, islamqa.info

---

## RIBA (Interest/Usury) — Strictly Prohibited

### Types of Riba
1. **Riba Al-Fadl** — An unequal exchange of ribawi commodities of the same type (e.g., gold for gold of unequal weight, wheat for wheat in unequal quantity)
2. **Riba An-Nasi'ah** — An addition charged in exchange for a delay in debt repayment; this is the basis of conventional bank interest

### Modern Applications
- Conventional bank savings accounts and loans bearing interest = **haram**
- Credit card interest charges = **haram**
- Installment sale with a price increase: **permissible** if the final price is fixed and agreed upon at the time of the contract (some ikhtilaf exists; this is the majority Salafi position)

**Key fatwa:** Lajnah Da'imah no. 4011; islamqa.info fatwa no. 22905

---

## GHARAR (Excessive Uncertainty) — Prohibited in Contracts

- Commercial insurance (Ta'min Tijari) = **haram** due to gharar (uncertainty) and maysir (gambling-like elements)
- Exception: Takaful (Islamic cooperative insurance) — permissible if structured correctly according to Shari'ah principles
- Futures trading involving commodities that cannot be delivered at the time of contract = generally **haram**

---

## AKAD (Contracts) — Conditions for Validity

An Islamic contract requires:
1. A clear offer (ijab) and acceptance (qabul)
2. Legally competent parties
3. A known and defined subject matter — no excessive uncertainty (gharar)
4. A permissible purpose and object

## COMMON MODERN FINANCIAL QUESTIONS

### Multi-Level Marketing (MLM)
- If income primarily derives from recruitment fees rather than actual product sales = **haram** (maysir and gharar)
- If structured around legitimate products with commissions tied to real sales = may be permissible; requires analysis of the specific scheme
- **Reference:** Ustaz Erwandi Tarmizi (PengusahaMuslim.com)

### Cryptocurrency
- Scholarly ikhtilaf exists; most Salafi scholars advise caution or prohibition due to:
  - Highly speculative nature (resembles maysir)
  - No intrinsic commodity backing
  - Extreme gharar (price volatility and uncertainty)
- **Reference:** islamqa.info fatwa no. 258320; positions vary among contemporary scholars

### Dropshipping / E-Commerce
- Selling goods one does not yet own before taking possession: scholarly ikhtilaf
- Salam contract (advance payment for specifically described future goods): **permissible** with defined conditions
- **Reference:** KonsultasiSyariah.com; islamqa.info

### Stock Market Investing
- Purchasing shares in companies whose primary business is halal = **permissible**
- Purchasing shares in companies primarily engaged in haram activities (conventional banking, alcohol, tobacco, etc.) = **haram**
- Short-selling = **haram** (selling what one does not own)

## ZAKAT ON BUSINESS ASSETS

- **Zakat Al-Tijarah (Trade Zakat):** 2.5% on net zakatable trade assets held for a full lunar year at or above the nisab threshold
- Nisab reference: equivalent to 85 grams of gold or 595 grams of silver — use the silver standard as it is more conservative and protective for zakat recipients
